About the role

VP Students

The VP Students manages graduate student programs and services, including budgeting, social events, advocacy, and collaborations with Affiliate Organizations and partners.

🎉
Social Events

Community & Events

Plans and organizes social events, programs, workshops, and classes for graduate students across UBC, as well as networking opportunities with the broader grad community.

🤝
Partnerships

Affiliate Organizations

Manages relationships with GSS Affiliate Organizations (AOs), coordinates funding applications, event promotion through GSS channels, and collaborative opportunities.

📋
Administration

Programs & Services

Oversees budgeting and delivery of graduate student programs and services, including the AO funding pool of $1,000 per organization per academic year.

📣
Advocacy

Student Advocacy

Advocates for graduate student interests and ensures graduate student voices are represented in planning events and programs that serve the wider community.

GSS Committees

Committee Involvement

The VP Students works alongside the following GSS committees, each of which is open to all UBC graduate students.

Elections & Engagement Committee

Manages the Society's referendums and annual elections, plans social events, programs, workshops, classes, and networking opportunities for grad students.

Biweekly / Monthly

Academic & External Affairs Committee

Lobbies important academic and personal issues to the University and externally, and conducts research such as the annual GSS Student Satisfaction Survey.

Bi-monthly

House Finance Committee

Directs and manages the financial wellbeing of the Society and oversees the use of facilities at the Graduate Student Centre at Thea Koerner House.

Biweekly

Human Resources Committee

Ensures the Society is staffed with a strong team, monitors staffing needs, and oversees staff recruitment and performance evaluations.

Bimonthly

Governance & Accountability Committee

Engages in strategic planning, reviews the governance structure of the Society, and oversees the performance of all other committees.

AO-Funded Events

Each Affiliate Organization receives up to $1,000 per academic year to host social, cultural, academic, and professional development events. Events must be documented with attendance sheets, receipts, and a post-event report submitted within two weeks to receive reimbursement. Note that AO funds cannot be used to cover alcohol.

Affiliate Organizations

AO Program

Affiliate Organizations (AOs) work with GSS to access funding, event promotion, and campus-wide collaboration, helping connect and support UBC's graduate student community.

What AOs Get

The GSS partners with UBC department organizations, graduate communities, and select off-campus groups. As an AO, your organization gains access to GSS resources and support.

📢 Newsletter & social promotion 💰 Up to $1,000 event funding/year 🤝 Collaborative event opportunities 🎓 Leadership workshops 📡 Discord community access

How to Become an AO

1

Complete the Application Form — Find the AO application form, fill it out, and email it to vpstudents@gss.ubc.ca and aa@gss.ubc.ca.

2

Gather Supporting Signatures — Include four supporting signatures plus the primary contact (use an official email, not personal).

3

Confirmation Email — Expect a reply within a week. If not, send a follow-up — emails can occasionally be missed.

4

Attend Council Meeting — Attend the next Council meeting to address any questions. If no objections, your affiliation is approved!

GSS Council

Council

The GSS Council is the primary governing body of the GSS, made up of elected departmental graduate student representatives together with the GSS Executive and graduate student senators.

Monthly Council Meetings

Council meets on the third Thursday of every month in the Michael Kingsmill Forum (Room 4301) in the AMS Student Nest, from 5:30 – 7:30 pm. All meetings are open to graduate students and honorary members of the GSS to attend.
